Ifrane
Beautiful and peaceful place to spend your winter holidays, espicially if you're a fan of snow. Though Ifrane is very much known for its natural beauty, there is more to it than waterfalls and forests. There is a mausoleum in the city that dates back to the 16th century.
Agadir
Agadir is the beach resort of Morocco. It is a relatively small place, and therefore easy to get around on foot although there are also plenty of taxis too. Agadir is renowned for its beautiful stretch of sand. Unlike the beach at nearby Essaouira.
Bin Louidane
The Bin el Ouidane Dam is one of the biggest barrages in Africa , situated 28 kilometres south of Beni Mellal. The building process of the project was finished in 1953. This arch dam, which is said to be the highest one of its kind in Africa and the largest in Morocco
Essaouira
Bright and breezy Essaouira, on Morocco’s Atlantic coast , is an enchanting, laid-back alternative to the frenetic melting-pot of Marrakesh. The town’s name means “little picture” in Arabic, and it’s a painter’s dream location,
Rabat
Rabat has preserved traces of its rich past. Visit the Chellah, vestiges of the Roman Sala Colonia and the royal necropolis, shared by storks; admire Hassan Tower , an unfinished minaret attesting to a grandiose project designed by Sultan Yacoub el-Mansour.
Tangier
Once a glittering and debauched destination for the 1960s literary and artistic set , Tangier holds an evocative tinge of this past, even though it may no longer be the haunt of authors and painters. The medina area is the major point of interest to catch some of this atmosphere.
